35 YEARS OF NRL

How the National Rugby League has evolved over the years

By James Deng · Monash University · May 2026
PART ONE

The Game and Its Grounds

From the chaos of the 1990s Super League war to a continent-spanning competition in 2025, the NRL has grown into Australia's most-watched winter sport.

Rugby league has been played in Australia since 1908, but the modern era we see today is a national, popular, widely broadcasted competition, built over the last thirty-five years. In 1990 the New South Wales Rugby League fielded sixteen mostly-Sydney clubs. In 1998 the NRL was born from the wreckage of the Super League war. By 2025 the league runs seventeen teams across three Australian states, the ACT and New Zealand, with the Dolphins as its newest entry.

1Where the league is based

Each circle marks a club's home city, sized by total NRL matches played at that ground (1998–2025). The league's centre of gravity is unmistakably Sydney, where eight clubs share the harbour city, with secondary hubs in Brisbane, Newcastle, the Gold Coast, Auckland, and Melbourne.

2Club lifespans, 1990 – 2025

Each bar marks the years a club competed in the top tier of Australian rugby league. The mid-1990s Super League war drove a wave of mergers and exits, with eleven clubs leaving the competition since 1990, while expansion has added Brisbane, Melbourne, the Gold Coast, North Queensland, New Zealand, and most recently the Dolphins. Numbers beside each bar count first-grade premierships won.

PART TWO

How the Game Has Changed

Higher scores, faster games, and Friday-night kickoffs designed for prime-time TV, the NRL of 2025 plays a different shape of football to the NRL of 1998.

Twenty-eight seasons of match data, covering every game, every score, and every kickoff time, let us examine the long-run rhythms of the NRL. The window captures the 1998 unification, the post-2000 expansion, the 2020 COVID-truncated season, and the dynastic Panthers era of the early 2020s. The 2025 figures here are partial; the rest of the seasons are complete.

3Points per match by season

Average total points scored per match each year, a measure of how open the game has become.

4Home vs away scoring

Average points scored by home and away teams each season. The shaded gap between the lines is the home-ground edge, steady but consistently slim.

5The shape of a scoreline

Density of team-scores across six five-year eras, all overlaid on a single axis with a gold-to-green time gradient (oldest era in gold, newest in deep green). Most performances land between 10 and 30 points, and the long tail past 40 has thickened slightly — the deep-green 2023–2025 curve sits visibly above the gold 1998–2002 curve from about the 35-point mark onward.

6Kickoff times, when the NRL plays

A heatmap of match count by day of week and kickoff hour. Over 28 seasons the broadcast schedule has crystallised three blocks: a Friday night prime-time game, a Saturday afternoon family slot, and a Sunday afternoon closer.

PART THREE

Teams and Their Reign

Which clubs have dominated, which have travelled the furthest, and how the ladder has shifted from season to season.

A grand final tells you who won the premiership. The story of an NRL era, twenty-eight years of cross-country flights, narrow losses, and head-to-head rivalries, needs more than one chart to tell. The following six visualisations profile the league's clubs over the 1998–2025 window: total wins, travel burden, ladder volatility, head-to-head matchups, the relationship between travel and performance, and an at-a-glance team fingerprint.

7Total wins, 1998 – 2025

Foundation clubs Roosters, Storm, and Broncos lead the era. New entrants Titans and Dolphins sit lower simply by having played fewer seasons.

8Interstate trips made

A lollipop counting the matches each club played outside its home state. The Warriors are an outlier, with every road game being a flight across the Tasman.

9Three trajectories, 2014 – 2024

Regular-season ladder finish over the last full decade. Rank 1 is the minor premiership; rank 17 is the wooden spoon. The Storm's purple line barely leaves the top four. The Panthers begin mid-table and climb to four straight grand finals. The Bulldogs slide from finals contention to the wooden spoon. The other fourteen clubs are shown in grey for context.

10Head-to-head matrix

Home win-rate by fixture. Darker = the row team won more often at home against the column opponent.

11Does travel hurt?

Each dot is a club. Interstate trips on the x-axis, win-rate on the y-axis. Distance does not predict failure.

12Club fingerprints

Five metrics for the eight strongest currently-active clubs by overall win-rate (1998–2025): attack (avg points scored), defence (avg points conceded, inverted so longer = better), finals games played, home win-rate, and away win-rate.

About this visualisation

Created by James Deng for FIT2179 Data Visualisation, Monash University, Semester 1 2026. Published May 2026.

All twelve charts were built with Vega-Lite; the page layout uses Pure.css. Typography pairs Bebas Neue, Playfair Display, and Inter.

Source code, datasets, and the Vega-Lite JSON specifications for every chart are available in the GitHub repository: github.com/cranberryjams/VIS-2.

Data sources

  • Match results, datasets/match_data.csv: historical Australian rugby league fixtures, filtered here to the NRL competition only (1998–2025, 5,435 matches). Sourced from the uselessnrlstats public dataset on GitHub.
  • NRL results with venue and region, datasets/nrldata.csv: supplementary fixture data including kickoff location and state/region, used to validate and enrich the main match dataset. Sourced from thecruncherau / NRL Results on Kaggle.
  • Club timeline and metadata, datasets/teams.csv: founding year, end year, home city, premierships, and brand colour for each NRL/ARL/NSWRFL club active between 1990 and 2025. Compiled from each club's public Wikipedia article.
  • World coastlines for the venue map, from vega-datasets / world-110m.

AI use

Anthropic's Claude was used to assist with HTML/CSS layout and Vega-Lite spec drafting. All data was sourced from the public datasets listed above; no chart data was generated by AI.
